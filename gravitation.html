Deep Space Gravitational Insights

The Equivalence Principle

Galileo Galilei and later Albert Einstein proved that in a vacuum environment, all bodies accelerate at exactly the same rate regardless of their mass. Toggling the object mass slider in this laboratory layout visually demonstrates this metric constraint.

Escape Velocity Limits

To completely break free from Earth's gravitational lock, an object requires a launch velocity threshold equal to $11.2\text{ km/s}$. On the Moon, due to a significantly reduced gravitational constant ($1.62\text{ m/s}^2$), escape velocity drops to just $2.38\text{ km/s}$.

Terminal Velocity in Atmospheres

This sandbox calculates free-fall variables within an ideal classical physics vacuum air loop. In the presence of air, objects encounter upward drag resistance forces. Acceleration drops continuously until drag matches weight, creating a fixed terminal speed velocity boundary.
